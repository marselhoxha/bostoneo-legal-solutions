Synthesis locked · stepper rendering open

All flow decisions are locked. Three ways to render the status stepper.

The full /tasks list-view + modal flow is locked: D2 row, centered modal opening via state-driven cache, time-log conditionally hidden when case.billingType = CONTINGENCY, BLOCKED required-prompt, subtasks CRUD, add-only comments, activity log collapsed by default. The only open visual question is how the status stepper renders. Three options below — each shows the SAME modal layout (Hoxha v. MGH, "Review medical records vol 3"), differing only on the stepper visualization at the top.

  • V1 — Segmented Pills: 4 connected pill segments inside a single rounded container. Past steps green-tinted with check; active step accent-tinted with subtle border ring; future steps muted gray. Compact, reads as a "control".
  • V2 — Chevron Flow: 4 horizontal cells with chevron-pointed right edges, like a process workflow. Past = green fill; active = solid accent fill; future = neutral. Reads as "lifecycle" / "stages".
  • V3 — Track + Indicators: a horizontal progress track with 4 circular step indicators sitting on it. Filled portion of the track shows progress through the lifecycle. Step labels under each circle. Reads as "progression" / "milestone."
Variation 1

Segmented Pills — compact, control-like

Reads like a familiar segmented control (think OS-level toggle group). Compact at any width. Active step gets a subtle accent ring. Past steps tinted green with a small ✓. Cheapest to implement (~80 lines). Best for users who think of status as a "setting", not as a journey.
