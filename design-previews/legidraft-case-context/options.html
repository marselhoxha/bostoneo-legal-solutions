LegiDraft · Case Context Redesign

Four ways to place case context without the 320px right rail.

The standalone Case Context panel eats ~320 px of chrome and shows "No case linked" most of the time. Each option below collapses that affordance into UI that scales with its content — small when empty, informative when filled. All mocks use production dark-mode tokens; click any mock's empty → linked pair to compare.

Current pain: right rail renders a full-sized card for the empty "No case linked" state, duplicates case info already shown on Step 4's Summary card, and adds a second place the attorney has to manage case linking.

A

Top-bar strip inside the wizard Recommended

A thin status row at the top of the wizard card, above the stepper. Empty state is ~40 px; filled state expands to ~52 px with pills. The right rail is removed entirely — the wizard becomes full-width (~1000 px on desktop), giving the practice-area grid more breathing room.

Strengths
  • Zero wasted real estate — strip shrinks to ~40 px when empty.
  • Wizard becomes full-width; PA grid reads like the approved mockup.
  • Case pills (PI · MA) give instant confirmation the AI has the right context.

  • Links against the mocked mockup's approved 2-column layout; departs from Sprint 4a spec.

C

Step-contextual — quiet chip + Step 4 form field

Steps 1–3 show a small quiet chip in the top-right of the wizard indicating link state. Case linking is a first-class form field on Step 4 (Review), where the attorney naturally confirms all inputs before Generate. No persistent panel.

Strengths
  • Case linking becomes a form field, not ambient chrome — stronger conceptual model.
  • Wizard card is at its most minimal in Steps 1–3 (just the stepper + content).
  • Quiet chip is optional — can be hidden on Steps 1–3 entirely for maximum calm.

Tradeoffs

  • Chip on Steps 1–3 is tiny — low information density if the attorney wants to double-check.
  • Post-wizard generation view loses the Case Context panel entirely.

Strengths
  • Smallest empty-state footprint of any option.
  • Full case detail on demand — rivals the current rail's density.
  • Always in the same spot; no layout shift between empty ↔ linked.
  • Feels contemporary (Linear / Notion-style meta chips).
Tradeoffs
  • Case context is hidden until hover — low glanceability.
  • Mobile-awkward (no hover); needs tap-to-toggle with outside-click dismiss.
  • Popover z-index conflicts with dropdowns inside the wizard (e.g., jurisdiction select).
My recommendation

Go with Option A. Fall back to B if simpler feels better.

Why A wins: it solves the original Sprint 4a rationale (“attorneys want visible confirmation that they are drafting against the correct case”) with 40–52 px of chrome instead of 320 px. The pills (PI · MA) give instant confidence; the Change/View actions handle the entire case-link lifecycle inline; and the full-width wizard finally lets the practice-area grid breathe the way the original mockup intended.

When B might win: if you find even the strip feels like visual noise and want the wizard card to be completely undecorated, B pushes all meta up to page-level breadcrumb. Less discoverable, but cleaner.

What to drop: the 320 px app-case-context-panel right rail. Moving case context into the wizard means the Step 4 Summary card (which already shows case info) becomes the final pre-Generate confirmation — which is what it was built to be.
